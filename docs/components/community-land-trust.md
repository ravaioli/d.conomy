# Community Land Trust (CLT)

## Description

A **nonprofit that holds land in trust** for community benefit. Housing, farms, and commercial spaces are leased at affordable rates, with ownership permanently removed from speculative markets.

## Governance

- CLT boards are composed of resident lessees, community members, and public representatives.
- CivicNotes serves as the public forum for CLT decisions (e.g., lease renewals, new acquisitions).
- All CLT documents are published on CivicNotes for transparency.

## Economic Integration

- CLTs can accept **Φ‑currency** for lease payments.
- They can issue “land stewardship bonds” to fund acquisitions, repaid via lease revenue.
- CLT formation itself can be a Proof‑of‑Regeneration event, minting Φ‑currency.

## Example User Flow

1. A Guild petitions to create a CLT for a blighted property.
2. After community approval, the CLT acquires the property using a combination of mutual credit, grants, and Φ‑currency.
3. The CLT develops affordable housing and leases units to members.
4. Lease payments (in Φ) fund maintenance and future acquisitions.
5. The CLT’s activities are tracked in the Φ‑Grid, improving the local “Responsiveness” score.

## Technical Implementation

- **Legal entity** registered as a nonprofit.
- **Smart contract** (optional) for lease payments and bond issuance.
- **Integration** with CivicNotes for public decision logs.
