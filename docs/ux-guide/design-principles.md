# UX Design Principles

d.conomy’s user experience is built on the same principles as CivicNotes: **legibility, relationality, and accountability**.

## 1. Legibility

- All economic flows are visible. Users can see:
  - Their Φ‑currency balance and transaction history.
  - How many times a tool has been borrowed.
  - The current demurrage rate.
- Use natural, biophilic visual language (see CivicNotes Φ‑Score design).

## 2. Relationality

- Economic interactions are presented as **relationships**, not just transactions.
- Mutual credit circles show a network graph of who owes whom.
- LoT borrowing history can show who else used the same tool, encouraging connection.

## 3. Accountability

- Every economic decision (e.g., minting new Φ) is tied to a CivicNotes proposal.
- Users can see who proposed a policy, who voted for it, and why.
- The Promise Tracker links PoR events to the original regenerative action.

## 4. Simplicity

- New users should be able to borrow a tool or join a mutual credit circle in under 3 clicks.
- Complex concepts (demurrage, mutual credit) are explained with tooltips and progressive disclosure.
